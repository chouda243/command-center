## 1. Crit-Rate Info Deck

### The Problem

After a decade in the games media industry, the founders found a critical oversight in how players discover and purchase games: aggregate sites, media outlets, and critics fail to account for player taste. They pivoted their entire games media brand to deliver highly targeted, taste-oriented recommendations, then realized the solution would benefit not only players but the people making games.

### Executive Summary

Crit-Rate is a platform designed to optimize growth across the entire gaming industry, from helping players find the right games to helping game makers find the right audiences.

**What we've built:**
- Fully activated community
- 3M+ data points
- 10K+ members

**What we do:**
- Audience-specific surveying and playtesting
- Target audience identification
- Personalized game recommendations

Positioning shorthand: a "Myers-Briggs" for gamers. Crit-Rate helps players find games and games find players.

### How It Works

Two linked outputs power the platform:

- **Gamer Segmentation (data-driven insights):** Crit-Rate segments users by personality characteristics, gaming motivations, and other critical identifying attributes. These segments band like-minded gamers into communities that fuel their personalized game recommendations.
- **Tailored Reviews:** While players find games through tailored reviews, developers and publishers get insights to position games to the right players faster.

### Value Proposition by Audience

Crit-Rate lifts all levels of the gaming industry:

- **Consumers:** personal identity, like-minded community, personalized reviews
- **Developers and Publishers:** direct access to the player base, surveying and focus groups, audience-specific playtesting, trend analysis, target audience identification, predictive modeling

### Capability Breakdowns (Four Services)

1. **Game Reports:** A comprehensive analysis service providing detailed insight on how current-gen games resonate with players. Crit-Rate analyzes player data and feedback to identify the types of gamers who enjoy specific games, pinpointing common traits and preferences that drive engagement.
2. **Audience Modeling:** In-depth data on a game's player personalities, gaming preferences, and motivational drivers to define the ideal player archetype. Targeting reveals specific insight into gameplay preferences and the social dynamics that influence enjoyment.
3. **Player Access:** Engage audience models based on gamer traits through focus groups, surveys, and direct lines of communication. The curated approach keeps player opinions relevant and tied to business decisions.
4. **Targeted Playtesting:** Curate a playtesting group of the right personality types to maximize accurate learnings. Crit-Rate runs sessions on a build of the game to generate gameplay recordings, observe player experiences, and build reports for further game and marketing development.

### Game Development Lifecycle Support

Crit-Rate plugs into every phase:

- **Define:** current-gen game reports, concepting target audience models, surveying players to gauge interest, roll-out with Crit-Rate
- **Design:** prototyping and game design doc, defining target audience models, focus groups on concept prototypes, pre-production playtesting
- **Develop:** production and QA testing, A/B testing on features and design, usability playtesting, pre-alpha / alpha / beta playtesting
- **Deploy:** marketing and built-in fans, seeding fan base via internal marketing, shape marketing campaigns, predictive modeling for targeted audiences
- **Refine:** maintaining feedback loops, post-launch surveying, updates / expansions / DLC planning, reception analytics and reporting

## 2. The Core Methodology (Trait Framework and Gamer Houses)

This framework underlies every game report and is shared by all three decks.

### How Data Is Collected (Three Steps)

1. Users take a personality survey asking about their gameplay style and habits.
2. Those answers correlate to 7 gamer trait scores that build a profile of the user.
3. Based on those trait scores, each user is matched to one of the Gamer Houses.

Each step yields insight into a player, from individual answers to trait scores to House placement. These points can be examined different ways to learn what players like in a game and what type of player resonated with a given game.

### The 7 Player Traits

Each profile consists of 7 criteria, each with two opposing tendencies. Everyone leans toward all traits but is usually more inclined toward one side. These traits also determine Gamer House placement.

| Trait | Tendencies | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Competitive** | For Fun / For Glory | How important winning is to enjoyment. Those who love the experience of playing are For Fun; those who seek the thrill of winning are For Glory. |
| **Players** | Solo / Multi | Preference for playing with others. Those who take in everything a game offers by themselves are Solo; those who love interacting with others are Multiplayer. |
| **Challenge** | Easy / Hard | Desire for difficulty. Those who play for the experience, not the frustration, sit on the Easy side; those who always take the road least traveled are on the Hard side. |
| **Creativity** | Practical / Experimental | How a player progresses. Those who forge ahead with purpose are Practical; those who wander and find their own objective are Experimental. |
| **Strategy** | Planning / Spontaneous | How a player approaches a situation. Those who gauge before acting are Planning; those who react and adjust on the fly are Spontaneous. |
| **Immersion** | Mechanic / Story | What absorbs a player most. Those who sink into the feel and flow of gameplay are Mechanic-focused; those swept away by the world and characters are Story-focused. |
| **Pace** | Slow / Fast | How a player prefers to engage. Those who savor the moment are Slow; those who want to progress and make decisions quickly are Fast. |

### The 5 Gamer Houses

Personality segments players are sorted into after the test. Each House captures HOW players enjoy playing rather than what they play, and is used to bind like-minded gamers together and learn from how they rate each game.

**Vantagist** — Strategy-focused players who enjoy planning and the achievement of overcoming difficult challenges. They favor slower-paced games that allow methodical thinking and problem solving.
- Trait highscores: Mid For Glory, Mid Solo, High Hard, High Planning, Mid Slow-Paced, Mid Mechanics

**Curionaut** — Exploration and experimentation-focused players who enjoy variety that lets them express and imprint their ideas onto the game. They gravitate to slower-paced games that encourage tinkering.
- Trait highscores: High For Fun, High Solo, Mid Easy, High Experimentation, High Slow-Paced, High Story

**Ascendiary** — Achievement-focused, completionist players with high dedication to beating games thoroughly. Relatively competitive, but they compete with themselves and the game rather than others.
- Trait highscores: Mid For Glory, Mid Solo, High Practical, Mid Spontaneous, Even in Slow/Fast-Paced

**Galabander** — Social gamers who like less competitive experiences they can share with friends. They enjoy the creative aspects of games and solve problems with others as a unit.
- Trait highscores: Mid For Fun, High Multiplayer, Mid Experimentation, Mid Planning, Mid Fast-Paced

**Bravadier** — Highly competitive gamers who enjoy high-intensity games as sport. They want to play with others, prefer a faster pace, and take the most practical path to victory.
- Trait highscores: High For Glory, High Multiplayer, Mid Hard, High Practical, High Fast-Paced, Mid Mechanics

**Ideal players, top genres:** Adventure (23%), Role Playing (18%), Action (15%), Horror (8%), Strategy (7%), Shooter (6%)

### Spending Analysis and Ways to Dive Deeper

With the ideal archetype defined, Crit-Rate can query further into habits, likes, and dislikes. Within the population of players who enjoyed the game, they were asked how much they would spend.

- **90% of players are willing to spend $20 to $30** on *Sorry We're Closed* based on the demo.
- As traits move from For Fun toward For Glory, Easy toward Hard, and Mechanics toward Story, players are willing to spend more.
- Core takeaway: harder challenges and story elements should be the primary marketing focus.

### Player Review Validation

What playtesters said and how their traits correlate:

- **Story immersion is key.** Players who enjoyed the game highlighted narrative depth, atmosphere, and emotional engagement. Correlation: higher Story immersion scores tend to leave glowing reviews.
- **Balanced challenge appeals.** Many praised the balance of difficulty as "rewarding" or "engaging." Correlation: these players had higher Challenge scores, but moderate on average, favoring games that are neither too easy nor too punishing.
- **Preference for solo experiences.** Users enjoyed experiencing the game fully solo. Correlation: low Players scores show primarily solo gamers who value independence.

### Target Audience Summary

**Primary audience:** *Sorry We're Closed* appeals primarily to solo players who value deep immersion, challenging gameplay, and meaningful narratives. They enjoy games that test their skills and reward persistence, favor progressing at their own pace, and are drawn to rich storytelling, detailed worlds, and mechanics that require thoughtful decision-making.

**Personality traits:**
- Solo-Oriented: prefers individual progression and personal achievement over social or multiplayer interaction
- Challenge-Seeking: enjoys higher difficulty and gameplay that rewards strategy and skill mastery
- Immersion-Focused: captivated by narrative depth, character development, and world-building
- Moderately Creative: appreciates exploring or experimenting within structured boundaries

**Player motivations:**
- Mastery: to overcome challenges and achieve personal milestones
- Exploration: to discover the game's story, lore, and hidden features
- Immersion: to lose themselves in a captivating world with rich characters and narratives
- Strategic Thinking: to engage in mechanics that reward careful planning and decision-making

**Marketing considerations:**
- Immersion: use cinematic storytelling, deep lore, and engaging characters in trailers and promo material
- Challenge: target challenge-driven players by showcasing active, skill-based mechanics
- Solo Play Appeal: promote the game as a personal journey ideal for immersive single-player fans

### Informing Marketing Strategies

Marketing for *Sorry We're Closed* should not only highlight player preferences and traits but embody them in method and messaging.

**Tailored direction:**
- Make every marketing touchpoint, from press to social media, feel like part of the game's world
- Avoid generic descriptions; immerse the audience in the language, tone, and mystery of the game
- Create a sense of curiosity and belonging, as if the story is incomplete without the player
- Structure trailers like a narrative teaser so viewers feel they are stepping into the protagonist's shoes

**Copy considerations (wrap the player into the narrative immediately):**
- "Heartbreak comes for all of us eventually, but will it make you stronger, or will you allow an outsider to exploit it?"
- "Use your wit and skill to overcome deadly demons and prove yourself in this battle of love and desire."
- "Delve into a journey all your own as your actions and decisions help guide Michelle to either salvation or chaos."

**Gamify audience engagement:** a small "dating profile" quiz aligned with the game's theme and tone. Questions can range from practical dating to choose-your-own-adventure situations, answers can hint at in-game mysteries, and results can reference game features with "learn more" calls to action.

**Social media engagement** (rewarding with digital wallpapers or shoutouts):
- *Third Eye Thursdays*: "Open your third eye" and spot secrets in an in-game location, image, or short video
- *"Choose your fate" polls*: interactive, story-driven polls that encourage consistent engagement

## 4. Storebound: May Playtest (June 16, 2025)

A full audience-modeling and playtest report for the co-op horror game *Storebound*, based on its May playtest.

### Ideal Player Archetype

The traits embodying the ideal player for *Storebound*:

| Trait | Result | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Competitive | For Fun | Enjoys success but doesn't need to win; values fair play, progress, and personal achievement over dominating others. |
| Players | Multi | Higher preference for shared experiences; more engaged interacting with others cooperatively or socially, though solo is still enjoyable. |
| Challenge | Easy | Enjoys well-balanced challenge but shies from punishing difficulty; values a smooth, frustration-free experience where flow matters more than difficulty. |
| Creativity | Experimental | Appreciates flexibility and creative problem-solving; enjoys mechanics that reward different playstyles. |
| Strategy | Balanced Planned and Spontaneous | Flexible; can plan ahead when needed but is equally fine reacting in the moment. |
| Immersion | Story | Driven more by narrative, atmosphere, and characters than pure mechanics. |
| Pace | Slow | Slight preference for slower, measured pacing; favors depth over speed. |

### Net Promoter Score Analysis

A comparison between players who rated the game positively ("Good" or "Excellent") and those who strongly recommended it ("9" or "10"). High recommenders accounted for **22% of all positive raters**. Recommenders (evangelists) differ from general positive raters as follows:

- **Players (Multi +213%):** Recommenders are much more multiplayer and socially inclined; the strongest advocates clearly value social connection more.
- **Challenge (Hard +131%):** Recommenders are more tolerant and even appreciative of challenge; positive raters lean toward easier, more relaxed play while evangelists enjoy being tested.
- **Pace (Fast +363.2%):** Recommenders prefer a faster tempo; they enjoy forward momentum and meaningful decisions without stalling.
- **Immersion (Mechanics +92.4%):** Recommenders are less driven by story and atmosphere; the strongest advocates are more delighted by well-crafted mechanics and deeper system variety.

### Storebound Audience Synopsis

Based on current player feedback, the audience most excited about *Storebound* is not necessarily the traditional co-op horror crowd. They are more interested in immersion, mood, and light collaboration, not high-pressure coordination or difficulty.

### Target Audience Overview

**Player data suggests** the primary audience is players who value immersive, story-rich environments and light, cooperative gameplay.

**Your Storebound player:** drawn to experiences emphasizing atmosphere, exploration, and shared problem-solving over difficulty or competition. They enjoy games that are emotionally engaging and creatively open, offering tension and mystery without overwhelming pressure. A puzzle-driven horror game that invites teamwork and discovery would activate them most effectively.

**Potential opportunity:** While *Storebound* leans horror, it resonates most with players who appreciate thematic tension and atmosphere over intensity and challenge. Focusing on story-driven puzzles, accessible co-op, and vibe-forward horror makes the May Playtest audience a real market fit.

**Potential threat:** If the vision is for *Storebound* to become a more demanding, mechanically intense co-op horror experience, validation with a different player segment may be needed to ensure alignment.

### Core Traits and Motivations

**Top player traits (who your players are):**
1. **Immersion, atmosphere-forward:** more absorbed in story, setting, and thematic design than gameplay systems
2. **Competitive, For Fun explorers:** guided by curiosity, not conquest; success measured by engagement, not winning
3. **Challenge, tension-balanced:** enjoy suspense and mystery but prefer manageable rather than punishing stakes
4. **Players, social collaborators:** open to cooperative play when thoughtful and intuitive; dislike chaotic coordination

**Main motivators (what they look for):**
- Thematic cohesion: a game world that feels alive and emotionally resonant
- Low-friction social: intuitive co-op play free from tension or competitive hierarchies
- Exploration and clarity: guidance and feedback loops that reward curiosity without punishing mistakes
- Creative expression: moments where their actions, choices, or ideas shape the experience

**Less motivated by:**
- High skill ceilings or "get good" loops
- Intense competition or leaderboard-style mechanics
- Heavy tactical systems or complex micromanagement

### Playtest Survey Report

#### Question 1: What was your most frustrating moment or aspect of what you just played?

*Common frustration themes:* Controls and Interactions ("couldn't pick up items," "interactions were hard," "button confusion," often paired with difficulty or bugs); Bugs and Glitches ("crashes," "glitches," "broken mechanics"); Objective Clarity (unsure of the goal or how to progress); Challenge/Difficulty (too hard or too easy, often tied to expectations).

*Player traits by theme:*
- **Controls/Interactions:** high Easy and Slow scores; mechanical friction frustrates players who want to take their time and feel in control
- **Bugs/Glitches:** mechanically focused players (balanced immersion) with Planning strategies and low Pace
- **Controls + Bugs:** high Hard and Multiplayer scores; even tough or social players get frustrated when basic controls don't function
- **Controls + Challenge:** a rare cluster with extremely high Easy and very Slow scores; they crave ease and clarity
- **Bugs + Objective Clarity:** extremely Experimental players who want to explore and find their own path, blocked by bugs and unclear goals

#### Question 2: What was your favorite moment or aspect of what you just played?

*Common favorite themes:* Atmosphere/Aesthetic ("vibe," "aesthetic," "art," "feel"); Challenge/Enemies ("monster," "enemy," "fight," "challenge"); Exploration/Discovery ("wander," "explore," "discovery"); Mechanics/Systems ("gameplay," "mechanic," "feature"); plus aesthetic blended with challenge or exploration.

*Player traits by theme:*
- **Atmosphere only:** balanced overall, slightly Story-leaning; broad appeal across player types
- **Atmosphere + Challenge:** very low Competitive, slight Multiplayer, mildly Experimental, moderate Challenge; they like engaging challenge wrapped in rich aesthetics
- **Atmosphere + Challenge + Exploration:** more challenge-oriented, Story-focused, slightly Slow; they savor tough, beautiful worlds
- **Atmosphere + Exploration:** very immersion-heavy, highly Experimental, low Challenge, very Solo; they wander freely in beautiful, mysterious spaces and uncover lore
- **Atmosphere + Mechanics:** extremely low Challenge (Easy), low Creativity (Practical), low Immersion (Mechanics); a niche that loves aesthetic polish but values fluid, easy mechanics

#### Question 3: What was the game about / what were you doing?

*Key interpretation themes:* Escape/Mission; Co-op/Teamwork; Objective/Task-Based; Shopping/Store premise; Stealth/Hiding.

*Traits by interpretation:*
- **Co-op/Teamwork:** Multiplayer-focused and high Challenge; team-based, deep in shared problem-solving
- **Teamwork + Tasks:** slightly Solo and Easy; systems-driven solo players who notice teamwork but prefer clarity and structure
- **Teamwork + Store premise:** highly Experimental, very low Strategy and Pace; world-absorbing players who read meaning through environmental context
- **Teamwork + Stealth:** creative-heavy (Experimental) with high Multiplayer and Pace; fast-moving, socially alert players who outmaneuver threats cooperatively
- **Escape/Mission:** the largest group, relatively neutral with a slight Story lean and low Challenge; grasped the premise without fixating on systems

#### Question 4: If you could change, add, or remove anything, what would it be?

*Common requested themes:* AI/Enemy Tuning; Content/Variety (more items, abilities, level types); Communication/Team Tools (mic, voice chat, signals); Objective/Structure Tweaks.

*Traits by request:*
- **AI/Enemy Tuning:** slightly challenge-averse, near-neutral, mildly Story-oriented
- **AI + Content/Variety:** balanced with a slight Multiplayer lean and moderately Experimental; want meaningful variation in AI and systems
- **AI + Content + Communication:** high Strategy, more Practical, strongly Solo; think structurally and want polish, clarity, and better team mechanics
- **Communication Tools:** very low Challenge, strongly Story-immersed, Slow; narrative-driven social players wanting deeper communication, coordination tools, and puzzles
- **Content/Variety:** slight Multiplayer and Story lean with generally low Challenge; want more world and gameplay flavor, not difficulty

#### Question 5: Was there anything you wanted to do but couldn't?

*Unmet action themes:* Movement/Actions (jump, run, basic maneuvers); Communication; Controls/Interface (buttons, inputs, mic settings, binding); Combat/Interactions; Visibility/Navigation (closely overlaps with interface issues).

*Traits by unmet action:*
- **Movement/Actions:** fairly neutral, slight Story immersion and Slow pace; wanted basic tools to navigate more freely
- **Communication:** strongly Multiplayer and very low Challenge; casual cooperative players who play for fun with friends
- **Controls + Communication:** extremely Experimental, high Spontaneous, Fast; expect refined mechanics and quick interaction
- **Controls only:** high Experimental and Story immersion with very low Challenge and Slow pace; want a smoother traversal experience
- **Combat/Interactions:** mildly Experimental, slightly Story-driven, mostly avoid Challenge; want more active interaction without intense difficulty

### Playtest Wrap-Up: Crit-Rate's Learnings and Takeaways

Based on playtester trait data, opinions on the game, and survey responses.

**Core pillar: Atmosphere.** Across nearly every cluster, favorite moments combined vibe, tension, team-based presence, and world aesthetic. *Storebound* is achieving emotional glue through atmosphere alone. Every gameplay mechanic, and even clarity and instruction elements (what can I do, where should I go), should align with the tone and atmosphere and should not break immersion.

**Clarity is a friction point.** Most frustrations were not about challenge but about not understanding how or whether something could be done. Main barriers: tutorial pain, UI issues, unclear objectives, and interactions with no feedback. Players often felt the game wasn't responding as expected, which points to clarity around player intent versus system response.

**Need for more social tools.** Players highlighted teamwork, atmosphere, and shared survival but noted missing or frustrating communication features. Considerations: pinging and emoting, dead players talking among themselves, better location communication, and key bindings for push-to-talk.

**Mechanics and interactions.** Many players were surprised they couldn't do basic things like jump, speak, or interact more deeply with the environment. Considerations: jumping and climbing, and light environmental interactions (for example, an intercom system or security room cameras).
